          Press Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      July 14  2005   WASHINGTON  D C       U S  Senator Tom Coburn  M D   R OK   a member of the Senate Judiciary Committee  today announced his support for legislation  S  1313  that would limit the government   s ability to seize private property in the wake of a misguided U S  Supreme Court decision  In Kelo v  City of New London the Court ruled that government may seize the home  small business  or other private property of one owner and transfer that same property to another private owner  simply by concluding that such a transfer would benefit the community through increased economic development  The legislation sponsored by U S  Senator John Cornyn  R TX  would restore the meaning of the Constitution  which does not equate    public use    with    economic development          The recent Kelo decision is one of the most disturbing decisions to come out of the Supreme Court in modern times  This decision made a mockery of our Fifth Amendment private property rights  The Court   s reasoning in this case was judicial activism in its most dangerous form     Dr  Coburn said    The Fifth Amendment of the U S  Constitution states that    private property    shall not    be taken for public use without just compensation    and that private property can only be seized for    public use           Economic developers in countries like China who believe it is appropriate to seize private property for financial gain have now found common cause with five of the justices serving on the U S  Supreme Court  I would urge my colleagues to support this legislation that could help nullify this unprecedented  dangerous and unconstitutional land grab decision     Dr  Coburn said
