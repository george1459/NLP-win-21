          Press Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      March 3  2005  WASHINGTON  D C       U S  Senator Tom Coburn  M D   R OK  today voted for a measure  S J  Res  4  to block the United States Department of Agriculture  USDA  from reopening the U S  border to Canadian cattle  Coburn cast his    yes    vote after the USDA refused to consider a process that would have helped Oklahoma farmers and ranchers adjust to the reopening of the border        Opening the Canadian border overnight will flood our market with Canadian beef and lower prices for cattle producers in Oklahoma  The Canadian border should be reopened in a gradual  not abrupt  manner so cattle producers in Oklahoma and other states can have time to adjust to changing market conditions     Dr  Coburn said  adding that under the USDA   s proposed rule Canadian beef will not compromise the safety of our beef supply    Dr  Coburn proposed to Secretary of Agriculture Mike Johanns that the department implement a staged reintroduction of Canadian cattle by age over the next 12 months  but the Secretary did not adopt Dr  Coburn   s recommendations     Oklahoma cattle producers are especially vulnerable to a sudden and dramatic influx of Canadian cattle at this time because many producers graze their cattle on their winter wheat pasture  Oklahoma wheat cattle are typically taken off wheat pasture by March 10  about the time the Canadian border would reopen  If producers are unable to sell their cattle now because of dramatic price drops they may be forced to sacrifice that crop  further compounding their loss
