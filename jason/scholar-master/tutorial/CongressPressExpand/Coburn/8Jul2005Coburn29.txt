                                                                After three unsuccessful tries  Bartlesville veteran finally gets award July 8  2005  TULSA  OK       M D   R OK  today presented Bartlesville resident Oscar E  Boyd with the Korean War Service Medal for his honorable service to this country during the Korean Conflict       Mr  Boyd served our country honorably in Korea and it   s my honor to present this award to him today     Dr  Coburn said     Those who take up the cause of freedom and defend our great nation deserve our highest respect and gratitude       Oscar Boyd served in the U S  Army in the 1st Cavalry Division and the 7th Infantry Division   s 7th Quartermaster Company  He was honorably discharged May 8  1952  Mr  Boyd previously had unsuccessfully applied for the Korean War Service Medal     first awarded by the Republic of Korea on the 50th Anniversary of the Korean War on June 25  2000    The National Personnel Records Center told Mr  Boyd he lacked the necessary documentation to prove his service because the records were destroyed in a fire in the 1970s    Dr  Coburn   s staff was able to provide Mr  Boyd with the necessary proof by verifying his service through the National Archives through a    Morning Report     The report is a listing of all the members of an Army unit recording whether each soldier is fit for duty  missing in action or killed in action    In addition to the Korean War Service Medal  Mr  Boyd was presented with a letter of appreciation in English and Korean from the Republic of Korea       As a veteran  it   s comforting to know your service and sacrifice is appreciated and I want to thank Dr  Coburn and his staff for their help in securing this award     Boyd said
