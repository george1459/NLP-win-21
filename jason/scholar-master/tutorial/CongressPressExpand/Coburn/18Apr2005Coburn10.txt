                                                                 April 18  2005  WASHINGTON  D C       U S  Senator Tom Coburn  M D   R OK  today voted for S  256     The Bankruptcy Abuse Prevention and Consumer Protection Act of 2005     legislation that will provide long overdue reform to the nation   s bankruptcy laws  Since Congress last modified the nation   s bankruptcy laws nearly 30 years ago the number of the bankruptcy filings has increased eight fold  S  256 passed the Senate by a vote of 74 25     Under today   s bankruptcy laws those who don   t play by the rules impose a hidden tax on those who do  Unmet payments and debts aren   t forgiven  just passed on to other consumers  If this legislation becomes law consumers will pay lower interest rates and save hundreds of dollars every year     Dr  Coburn said  The legislation will protect individuals who need a fresh financial start by requiring payment of debts by individuals who have the ability to pay  The bill will also provide special protections for farmers  veterans  soldiers  nursing home patients and low income families  Individuals who will enjoy a safe harbor or exemption in the bill include people who earn less than the medium income in their state  low income veterans  debtors who have serious medical conditions or debtors who have been called or ordered to active duty military service  The bill includes other important consumer protections  Credit card companies will be forced to disclose the dangers of making only minimum payments and they will no longer be able to advertise low introductory rates in a deceptive manner     Filing for bankruptcy should be a last resort  not a first resource for people in a financial pinch  Unfortunately  loopholes in our bankruptcy laws have been exploited by speculative creditors and borrowers who  in some cases  plan for bankruptcy filings  Families facing a genuine financial crisis or medical emergency deserve a bankruptcy system that protects their rights  not today   s system that lets unscrupulous borrowers and lenders avoid financial responsibility at the expense of everyone else     Dr  Coburn said
