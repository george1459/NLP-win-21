          Press Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      June 14  2005  WASHINGTON  D C       U S  Senator Tom Coburn  M D   R OK  today praised the Bush administration   s plan to establish commissions to sunset and review federal programs  Clay Johnson  Deputy Director for Management in the Office of Management and Budget  OMB   announced the administration   s plan during a hearing before Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Subcommittee on Federal Financial Management  Government Information  and International Security  which Dr  Coburn chairs       President Bush   s new BRAC style proposal to sunset and review federal programs is precisely the kind of bold leadership that will help Congress control its unsustainable spending habits     Dr  Coburn said       One of the greatest impediments to the President   s vision of an ownership society is an inside the beltway entitlement society in which federal agencies expect ever increasing budgets regardless of their performance     Dr  Coburn said  pointing to the Office of Management and Budget   s Program Assessment Rating Tool  PART   which found that 61 percent of federal programs are not rated as either effective or moderately effective    Dr  Coburn added that while PART can help Congress prioritize within the budget  Congress itself must be much more aggressive about conducting oversight  Less than ten percent of all congressional hearings focus on oversight        Until Congress spends more time conducting oversight on the 1 200 federal programs we already fund we will never rein in spending  As a nation  we can no longer afford to make effectiveness and efficiency goals for next year   s Congress  We simply do not have the resources to fight terrorism and meet our obligations to programs like Social Security and Medicare  If we hope to address big challenges like Medicare and Social Security reform we need to act today to solve the smaller  but costly  challenges spread throughout the federal government     Dr  Coburn said
