          Press Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      May 19  2005  WASHINGTON  D C       U S  Senator Tom Coburn  M D   R OK  today challenged any member of Congress who supports destructive embryonic stem cell research to debate him at a mutually agreeable time     As a practicing physician and two time cancer survivor  I believe it is imperative that we pursue the most promising and ethical research  I have reviewed the available scientific data and it is clear that those pursuing destructive embryonic stem cell research are offering patients and their families false hope  Medical breakthroughs utilizing adult stem cells  cord blood cells and other ethical alternatives are occurring on a near weekly basis     Dr  Coburn said     Yet  there are no treatments or even promising human trials involving embryonic stem cells  I am troubled that supporters of destructive embryonic stem cell research are distorting science and distracting us from exploring areas of research that are yielding real results  Those results have already proven to hold the capability to improve the condition of patients suffering from a vast number of diseases and conditions  Before we divert more of our limited resources on hypothetical promises  we owe the public a real debate based upon science rather than emotion  false promises and wishful thinking  I therefore issue a challenge to any member of Congress who supports destructive embryonic stem cell research to debate the merits of this research versus the alternatives     Dr  Coburn said  In 2001  President Bush permitted federal funding research on the embryonic stem cell lines that were available at that time  Next week  the House of Representatives will consider the Stem Cell Research Enhancement Act of 2005 that would expand the amount of federal funds available to experiment on embryonic stem cells as well as the number of human embryos available for destruction
