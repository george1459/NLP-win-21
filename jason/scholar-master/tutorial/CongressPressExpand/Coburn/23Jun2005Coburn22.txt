          Press Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     GAO release new findings on Ryan White CARE program June 23  2005  WASHINGTON  D C       U S  Senator Tom Coburn  M D   R OK   a practicing physician  former co chair of the Presidential Advisory Council and HIV and AIDS  and Chairman of the Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Subcommittee on Federal Financial Management  held a hearing today examining the effectiveness of federal HIV AIDS CARE programs        Despite a massive expenditure of federal funds  we are losing ground in the battle against HIV AIDS     Dr  Coburn said     The rate of HIV infection is up and disturbing funding disparities have developed among minority and at risk communities across our nation       The General Accounting Office  GAO  released new findings at the hearing that revealed serious funding inequities in the Ryan White CARE Act  which Congress is scheduled to reauthorize this year  In 2000  then congressman Coburn was the primary author of the Ryan White CARE Act reauthorization  The GAO testified that   San Francisco is actually receiving federal funding for deceased AIDS patients  San Francisco receives almost double the funding per AIDS case than every other city eligible to receive Ryan White funds  Populations living in Southern states  particularly African Americans  are receiving disproportionately less funding  States such as Kentucky and West Virginia have reported the deaths of AIDS patients on waiting lists for the AIDS Drug Assistance Program due to financial shortfalls      AIDS is a global problem  The United States should be setting the standard for prevention and care  not setting an example to avoid  Congress should not wait any longer to implement prevention strategies that have been proven to work with other infectious diseases     Dr  Coburn said
