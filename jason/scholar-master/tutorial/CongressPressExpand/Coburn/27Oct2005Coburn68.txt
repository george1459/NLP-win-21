          Press Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Amendments save  15 million  include budget reforms October 27  2005  WASHINGTON  D C       U S  Senator Tom Coburn  M D   R OK  today applauded his colleague Arlen Specter  R PA   Chairman of the Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services  Education  and Related Agencies for accepting three of his amendments that will help Congress make wise financial decisions       Congress has a moral obligation to make difficult decisions about spending priorities as we fight the war on terror  recover from natural disasters and struggle to shore up Medicare and Social Security  I appreciate Senator Specter   s collegiality and courtesy as we discussed three of my amendments that I believe will help achieve this goal  I   m delighted that we were able to avoid a protracted floor debate about these reasonable measures     Dr  Coburn said    The first Coburn amendment that was accepted casts sunshine on the process of inserting earmarks     or pork    into appropriations bills  Previous Senate procedures allowed the Senate to automatically approve earmarks or special projects included in the House version of an appropriations bill without actually having to be considered or contained within bills or their accompanying reports  This amendment helps life the veil of secrecy surrounding earmarks and will make Senators more accountable to the public    The second Coburn amendment that was accepted reduced the travel and conference budget for the Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  by  15 million  HHS spending on conferences has increased 50 percent since 2000  which is inappropriate in a time of war and rising deficits  HHS sent 100 or more employees to at least 59 conferences in 2004  including 1 036 to a conference in Orlando  Florida  In the modern telecommunications era  it is unnecessary to spend time and resources to finance so many conferences     The third Coburn amendment that was accepted directed the Secretary of HHS and the Secretary of Education to estimate their improper payments as required by law  Improper payments     which include inadvertent  fraudulent  and irresponsible payments     cost the taxpayers at least  45 billion each year  Even worse  this  45 billion represents only 17 of 70 agencies that are currently reporting improper payment information as required under law    The Medicare program  which is already reporting  makes up nearly half      21 7 billion     of the government   s  45 4 billion reported improper payments for fiscal year 2004  The magnitude of the government   s improper payment problem is not yet known because some of the largest programs are not reporting  as required by law     Dr  Coburn also offered an amendment to transfer  60 million from renovations and building construction at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention  CDC  to the AIDS Drug Assistance Program  ADAP   Although Congress has appropriated more than seven times the President   s request for CDC construction  the Senate defeated the amendment by a vote of 85 to 14
