          Press Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      June 28  2005  WASHINGTON  D C      U S  Senator Tom Coburn  M D   R OK  today helped pass H R  6  the    Energy Policy Act of 2005     by a vote of 85 12        This bill will help reduce our dependence on foreign sources of energy while creating jobs here at home  Key provisions in this bill will help lower the cost of natural gas and limit the rise in gasoline prices  Our national and economic security will be strengthened by this comprehensive and long overdue legislation     Dr  Coburn said       Oklahoma stands to benefit from this plan     Dr  Coburn added     I   m pleased that this legislation contains incentives to encourage deep gas drilling  as well as other provisions that will help Oklahoma remain a leader in the energy sector  Western Oklahoma  in particular  with its abundant natural gas reserves  can play a critical role in stabilizing natural gas markets and lowering prices for home heating  electricity generation  and fertilizer for our farmers       The    Energy Policy Act of 2005    includes the following key provisions    A strong federal program to design and deploy clean coal technologies so America can continue to use its 250 year supply of coal while protecting our air and water  Provisions to streamlining of oil and gas development on existing federal lease sites to bring the fuels to market sooner  An inventory of oil and gas resources on the Outer Continental Shelf that to enable to the federal government to better manage these resources  Tax incentives to increase the production of electricity from new advanced nuclear power plants  Tax incentives program to encourage the construction of clean coal facilities  including a new 20 percent investment tax credit for clean coal facilities and a new 20 percent investment tax credit for coal gasification units that produce fuels and chemicals
