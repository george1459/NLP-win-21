          Press Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      March 7  2005  WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Tom Coburn  M D   R OK  today voted against two well intentioned but misguided amendments to raise the minimum wage that would have eliminated jobs for young workers and other workers with limited skills and training     Raising the minimum wage is one of Washington   s acts of counterfeit compassion that hurts the people it intends to help     Dr  Coburn said  pointing to Bureau of Labor Statistics findings that minimum wage increases in 1990  1991  1996 and 1997 led to a decrease in employment among young workers  Coburn also cited a survey from the Journal of Economic Perspectives in which 71 percent of economists at America   s top universities stated that a minimum wage increases unemployment among young and unskilled workers     Debates about the minimum wage focus our attention on the wrong problem  The way to improve wages is not through class warfare rhetoric about    wealth gaps    but by enacting policies that close the    independence gap    that leads to economic disparity  If there are two Americas  one group of Americans is free to choose their retirement plan  their doctor and where to send their child to school  The other group of Americans is not free to make these choices because the federal government believes it should make these choices for them     Dr  Coburn said     It is ironic that many Senators who supported a minimum wage increase want to deny low income workers the right to own  at a greater rate of return  personal retirement accounts they can pass on to family members     Dr  Coburn added     Rather than micromanaging the free market and the lives of small business owners and their employees  Congress should enact policies that give these individual more control over their own lives and resources
