   Obama Statement on the 40th Anniversary of the Voting Rights Act   U S  Senator Barack Obama                       Barack Obama                                                                           Obama Statement on the 40th Anniversary of the Voting Rights Act Saturday  August  6  2005  FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  Washington Contact  Robert Gibbs or Tommy Vietor   202  228 5511  Illinois Contact  Julian Green     Date  August 5  2005  Obama Statement on the 40th Anniversary of the Voting Rights Act    Forty years ago  the Voting Rights Act of 1965 was made law by millions of ordinary Americans who showed the extraordinary courage to overcome       These heroes of our past didn t wait for their government to act or complain idly when it didn t   They believed in the promise first articulated by our founding fathers   that in America  we have it in our power to begin the world anew       And so they marched   Women who were willing to walk instead of ride the bus after a long day s work of doing somebody else s laundry and looking after somebody else s children because they believed in freedom   Young people of every race and every creed who were willing to take a bus down to Mississippi and Alabama to register voters even though some would never return   And the brave Americans who faced hate and violence one Sunday on the Edmund Pettus Bridge not a few months before the Voting Rights Act delivered them redemption       Since that day  the Voting Rights Act has been a critical tool in ensuring that minority voters and all Americans not only have the right to vote  but the right to have their vote counted   And so those of us concerned about protecting those rights can t afford to sit idly by when the law comes up for reauthorization   We must stand up  speak out  and create a united front as we seek to renew these freedoms in the months to come
