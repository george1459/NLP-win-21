                                                                                                                         SENATOR McCAIN CONDEMNS 12 YEARS OF IMPRISONMENT FOR AUNG SAN SUU KYI October 24  2007 WASHINGTON  DC  U S  Senator McCain today condemned the 12 year imprisonment of Aung San Suu Kyi  the leader of Burmas National League for Democracy and urged Congress to act by passing the Saffron Revolution Support Act    Today marks twelve cumulative years in captivity for Aung San Suu Kyi  Her resilience in the face of untold sufferings  her courage at the hands of a cruel junta  and her composure despite years of oppression inspire the world  I once had the opportunity to meet this remarkable woman  and I was heartbroken to see photos of her meeting earlier this month with U N  Special Envoy Ibrahim Ganbari  Serene as always  Aung San Suu Kyi appeared at home  away from her people  imprisoned by a thuggish and illegitimate regime   In crushing the Saffron Revolution  killing hundreds and jailing thousands  including countless Buddhist monks  the junta has left no doubt about its intentions  Nor should the world have any doubt about ours  That is why I was pleased with the financial sanctions announced by President Bush last week  and encouraged that Australia imposed similar targeted sanctions today  Both are critical elements of the comprehensive international response that the Burmese crisis demands   But they are not  on their own  enough  That is why I urge Congress to pass the S  2172  the Saffron Revolution Support Act  This bill  introduced last week  imposes targeted financial sanctions against the juntas leadership  broadens the ban on Burmese imports to cover gems and hardwoods  requires disinvestment by U S  companies investing in Burma  and provides funds to support democratic activists in the country    By passing this bill  and by working with our international partners to show the Burmese generals that their pattern of repression is unacceptable  we can augur a new day for the long suffering Burmese people  Aung San Suu Kyi  after 12 years imprisonment in her own land  deserves our greatest and most immediate efforts  So do all those she has the courage to lead                                             October 2007 Press Releases           Browse by
