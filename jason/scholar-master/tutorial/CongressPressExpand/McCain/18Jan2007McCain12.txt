                                                                                                                         SENATOR MCCAIN HAILS PASSAGE OF THE LEGISLATIVE TRANSPARENCY AND ACCOUNTABILITY ACT January 19  2007 Washington  D C   U S  Senator John McCain today released the following statement  applauding the passage of the Legislative Transparency and Accountability Act      I am pleased the U S  Senate has acted to address comprehensive lobbying  ethics and earmark reform in order to restore the confidence of the American people  Greater disclosure and transparency into the interactions of lobbyists with our public officials is overdue and will only lead to better government  Requirements to reign in wasteful spending by disclosing earmarks and requiring members of Congress to pay the full charter rate when flying on corporate aircraft  instead of simply reimbursing the first class rate as required under the current rules  will reassure the public that their elected representatives are doing the work they were sent to Washington to do without undue influence from special interests      As members of Congress  we owe it to the American people to conduct ourselves in a way that reinforces  rather than diminishes  the public   s faith and confidence in Congress  While I had hoped the legislation would have done more to curtail earmarking  we have taken a step in the right direction to help balance the right of the public to know with its right to petition government  the ability of lobbyists to advocate for their clients    causes with the need for truthful public discourse  and the ability of members to legislate with the imperative that our government must be free from corrupting influences  both real and perceived
